Touching the Clouds in Rio

There is an undeniable energy that hits you the moment you step off the train at the summit of Mount Corcovado. Standing at the feet of Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor), one of the Seven Wonders of the Modern World, you don't just see Rio de Janeiro—you feel it.

The statue, crafted in the Art Deco style and covered in thousands of soapstone tiles, has watched over the "Marvelous City" since 1931. From this height, the 360-degree views of Sugarloaf Mountain, Ipanema Beach, and the Maracanã Stadium are simply unparalleled.

Renata’s Influencer Tip

To get that iconic "wide-arm" shot without hundreds of tourists in the background, you must take the very first train up at 8:00 AM. Also, wear bright colors like yellow or white—they pop beautifully against the blue Rio sky and the grey soapstone of the statue.

Whether you choose to take the traditional cog train through the Tijuca Rainforest or the vans from Copacabana, the journey is just as beautiful as the destination. It remains one of my favorite highlights from my travels across South America.
